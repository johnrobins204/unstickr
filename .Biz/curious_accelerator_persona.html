Gamifies the experience (e.g., acting as AI "Dungeon Master") to keep things moving fast.
Visibly disengages (checks phone, sighs) during basic setup or slow typing phases.

Feels

Insatiably Curious: Deeply driven by wonder and the "how" of things.
Impatient/Restless: High cognitive energy; hard to sit still through slow parts.
Eager: Like a kid on Christmas morning, wanting to unwrap the tech *now*.

The Operating Speed Disconnect

His need for speed leads quickly to boredom and ineffective or abandoned engagement.

Design Mandate: We must give him a high-stimulation expecience on his own device, but tied to his child learning outcomes (ie Learn together at different depths).

Joy of Learning

He wants deep learning opportunities. The more niche the topic the better.

Design Mandate: Don't just build a tutor, design a wordlbuilder's toolkit for literacy

The Energy Mismatch

He is running at 100mph (conceptual); the child is at 10mph (mechanical execution).

Design Mandate: The parent interface needs high-frequency interaction (tweaking parameters, adding obstacles) while the child interface focuses on deep work.
