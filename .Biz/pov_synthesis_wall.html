We have synthesized the "swirling needs" of the ecosystem into 5 distinct Point of View (POV) statements for each persona.
Format: [USER] needs to [NEED] because [INSIGHT]

The Volatile Creator

Child (Age 7)

He needs a digital valve to release ideas slowly, because his mental flood of concepts causes a traffic jam that blocks him from starting.

He needs voice-first capture, because his physical hand speed cannot keep up with the velocity of his imagination.

He needs a "narrative container", because his ideas are so explosive that he loses the thread of the story.

He needs to isolate variables, because facing a blank canvas with infinite AI possibilities causes cognitive washout.

He needs immediate visual feedback, because he thinks in scenes and "movies" rather than in text sentences.

The Voice Protector

Teen (Age 14-18)

She needs "Idea Maps" not text generation, because she fears AI phrasing will overwrite her authentic human identity.

She needs incremental scaffolding, because she wants the pride of "doing the walking" herself.

She needs high-transparency tools, because she is experiencing "identity dread" and needs to know what is hers.

She needs critique, not correction, because she wants to improve her skill, not have a machine fix it.

She needs privacy controls, because she views her writing as a vulnerable extension of her self.

The Teacher

Educator (The Scaffold)

They need process analytics, because they can no longer trust the final essay as proof of understanding.

They need higher-level critique tools, because they must shift curriculum from "generating text" to "evaluating arguments."

They need "Impossible without AI" assignments, because they want to leverage the tech for complexity rather than ban it.

They need customizable constraints, because they need to force students to think critically.

They need to bridge the gap between colleagues, because they are carrying the burden of scaffolding this complexity alone.

The Curious Accelerator

Parent (Eager)

He needs a high-stimulation dashboard, because his need for speed often outpaces his child's need for slow learning.

He needs "collaborative parameters", because he wants to be an active participant, not just a supervisor.

He needs deep-dive learning links, because he is driven by the "joy of big concepts" and the underlying tech.

He needs separate interfaces, because his high-energy intervention can accidentally rob the child of "execution" work.

He needs "Worldbuilder Tools", because he is motivated by the complexity and potential of the technology itself.

Sarah

Parent (Process Purist)

She needs "artificial friction", because she believes deep learning only happens during the struggle of figuring it out.

She needs the Socratic Method (questions, not fixes), because her core fear is the "cognitive atrophy" of her child.

She needs process visibility, because she values the evolution of the draft more than the perfection of the result.

She needs "effort metrics", because she views instant AI generation as "cheating."

She needs to disable "magic buttons", because she prioritizes grammar and logic skills over creative output.

The Reluctant Guardian

Parent (Skeptic)

She needs zero-setup entry, because she fears looking incompetent and will abandon the tool if she encounters friction.

She needs "plain language" instructions, because she feels alienated by tech jargon.

She needs proof of effort, because she biases toward "real work" and views digital tasks as inherently lazy.

She needs analog bridges (printing, physical play), because she values nature and non-digital time above screen time.

She needs a clear "finish line", because she is a "hostage user" who wants the homework done so the family can disconnect.
