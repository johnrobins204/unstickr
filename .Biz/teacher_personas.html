Teacher Empathy Maps

Pedagogical Friction Points: 2024-2026

Early & Middle Years

Focus: Developmental & Tactile
The Tactile Guardian Grade 3
Battling cognitive & motor atrophy
Says
Put the iPads away. Today we are using scissors and glue, not prompts.
Thinks
If they never struggle to draw a circle, they will never understand geometry.
Does
Creates "Analog Zones" where digital tools are physically banned.
Feels
Protective. Like the last line of defense against a physically helpless generation.
Core Friction: Loss of Process
The Wonder Facilitator Grade 5 Arts
Managing infinite imagination vs. limited skill
Says
Okay, you made a dragon. Now, can you explain *why* you chose green scales?
Thinks
The output is beautiful, but did they learn anything about shading or perspective?
Does
Attempts to grade the "Prompting Journey" rather than the final artifact.
Feels
Conflicted. Excited by engagement, but worried about shallow learning.
Core Friction: Assessment of Creativity

Senior Years

Focus: Integrity & Verification
The Integrity Hawk Grade 11 English
Policing the "Transactional Cheat"
Says
This vocabulary doesn't match your in-class speaking style. Come see me.
Thinks
I am spending more time being a forensic detective than a teacher.
Does
Reverts to oral exams (Viva Voce) and in-class handwritten essays to ensure authenticity.
Feels
Exhausted. Distrustful. Feels the student-teacher bond is breaking.
Core Friction: Erosion of Trust
The Pragmatist Grade 12 Tech/Biz
Leveraging efficiency vs. critical thought
Says
Don't just copy the summary. Use AI to find the flaws in the argument.
Thinks
Banning it is useless. But they are using it to be lazy, not to be smart.
Does
Assigns "Impossible without AI" tasks, expecting higher-level critique.
Feels
Frustrated by colleagues who ban it, but disappointed by students who misuse it.
Core Friction: Scaffolding Complexity
