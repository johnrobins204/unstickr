AI & Creative Learning Personas

Mapping student perspectives across age groups to inform product design.

The Senior Years (Ages 14-18)

Theme: "The Tension of Integrity"

The Voice-Protector

The Anxious Writer

Primary Modality
Creative Writing Essay Composition
Core Friction

Identity dread. Fears that AI phrasing will "overwrite" their authentic human fingerprint.

Design Needs

Incremental scaffolding. High-transparency "Idea Maps" rather than full-sentence generation.

"I want the AI to give me a map, but I want to be the one who does the walking."

The Style-Synthesizer

The Digital Artist

Primary Modality
Digital Illustration Graphic Design
Core Friction

Prompting fatigue. Frustrated by the lack of granular, tactile control over the machine's output.

Design Needs

Human-in-the-loop interfaces. Sketch-to-image tools and direct manipulation of AI layers.

"AI is just a better Photoshop brush. I just want it to look exactly how I imagined it."

The Tactile Traditionalist

The AI-Skeptic

Primary Modality
Clay & Physical Craft Painting
Core Friction

AI Perfectionism. Feels digital art is "soulless" and values the educational value of mistakes.

Design Needs

Process-oriented AI. Feedback that critiques and encourages physical work without replacing it.

"Perfect is boring. I like that my painting has a smudge—the AI would never do that."

The Social Pragmatist

The Collaborative Learner

Primary Modality
Video Editing Group Projects
Core Friction

Hallucination fatigue. Worries that AI errors will make them look incompetent to peers.

Design Needs

High-transparency tools. In-line fact-checking, source linking, and collaboration features.

"It's a smart friend. I use it to get the boring stuff out of the way so I can hang out with my real friends."

The Early & Middle Years (Ages 6-13)

Theme: "Magic & Shortcuts"

The Wish-Granter

The Dreamer (Ages 6-9)

Primary Modality
Storytelling Image Gen
Core Friction

Motor skills gap. Imagination is faster than their hands; frustration when they can't express complex ideas.

Design Needs

Conversational Interfaces. "Magic Wand" tools that interpret intent over precision.

"I told it a story about a cat, but it forgot the hat. Can you tell it to put the hat on?"

The Asset-Architect

The Modder (Ages 10-13)

Primary Modality
Gaming Coding/Modding
Core Friction

Creation speed vs. Playtime. Wants high-quality assets (textures, skins) immediately to use in-game.

Design Needs

Asset Generators. Tools that export directly to game engines (Roblox/Minecraft) seamlessly.

"I need a cool logo for my clan. I don't want to draw it; I just want to equip it."

The Cheat-Code Seeker

The Gamer (Ages 10-13)

Primary Modality
Homework Chore Removal
Core Friction

Repetitive tasks block fun. Sees schoolwork as an obstacle course to be speed-run.

Design Needs

Gamified Learning. Tools that turn "cheating" into "optimizing" by teaching the concept while solving it.

"Why do I have to write the summary? The bot knows the book already. I want to go play."

The Hands-On Builder

The Maker (Ages 6-11)

Primary Modality
Lego / Cardboard Physical Play
Core Friction

Screens feel flat. Loses interest in digital AI because they can't "touch" the result.

Design Needs

Blueprint Generators. AI that outputs instructions for the real world (origami, building blocks).

"I don't want a picture of a robot. I want instructions on how to build one out of these boxes."
